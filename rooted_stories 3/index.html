Thesis Question

How do invasive plant species affect the biodiversity, local economies, and ecosystem health of modern day Indigenous lands, and how can centering Indigenous environmental perspectives challenge oversimplified media narratives of invasive plant species?

This platform aims to contribute new knowledge by interrogating the intersection of ecological imperialism, climate change, and Indigenous environmental stewardship. Rather than positioning invasive species as purely harmful, this research recognizes invasive species as complex ecological and cultural phenomena shaped by historical colonialism, environmental racism, and evolving climate patterns. By amplifying underrepresented Indigenous voices and knowledge systems (e.g., relational science and kinship traditions) this thesis challenges dominant environmental narratives and informs more nuanced, culturally responsive management strategies.
